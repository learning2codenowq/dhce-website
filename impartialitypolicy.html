Governance

Impartiality Policy

DHCE is committed to making every certification decision on the basis of evidence alone, free from outside influence, commercial pressure, or conflict of interest.

Our Commitment

Independent, Evidence-Based Certification

DHCE recognises that impartiality is fundamental to the credibility of halal certification. Every decision we make must be grounded in objective evidence of conformity and free from any influence that could compromise its integrity.

To uphold this commitment, DHCE has established an independent Impartiality Committee whose role is to provide continued confidence to the community, our clients, and the wider halal industry that our certification decisions are always made on merit alone.

All financial and corporate dealings between DHCE and its clients are made available to the Impartiality Committee for inspection, and regular meetings are held to verify ongoing compliance across all areas of our operations.

The Committee

What the Impartiality Committee Does

The Committee is made up of individuals with strong educational, technical, and industry backgrounds. Its members represent the interests of consumers, trade, and the broader halal industry, and bring the analytical and investigative skills needed to review and verify the information presented to them.

Reviews all DHCE certification decisions for impartiality
Inspects financial and corporate dealings with clients
Holds regular meetings to ensure continued compliance
Serves as an independent guardian of DHCE core principles
Policy Commitments

What DHCE Is Committed To

The Impartiality Committee has established clear measures that govern how DHCE conducts its certification activities. These commitments apply to all staff, auditors, and management without exception.

Full Disclosure of Conflicts

All DHCE auditors are required to identify and disclose any situation that could compromise their impartiality, including any prior or current relationship with a client under review.

No Personal or Commercial Ties

DHCE staff and auditors have no personal connections to the companies they certify, and hold no commercial or financial interests in any certified entity.

No Activities That Threaten Impartiality

DHCE will not undertake any activity that could endanger its impartiality. Where a situation creates an unacceptable impartiality risk, the certification activity will not proceed.

No Consulting Conflicts

DHCE will not provide certification services to any client where a relationship with a consulting company could create an impartiality risk or give rise to a conflict of interest.

Independent Funding

DHCE does not receive any financial support, directly or indirectly, from any source other than the service charges paid by its clients. Our operations are fully self-funded.

Management Commitment

DHCE's top management is fully committed to compliance with this Impartiality Policy and to promoting a culture of transparency and honest operations throughout the organisation.

Committee Composition

Who Sits on the Impartiality Committee

The Impartiality Committee is made up of individuals selected for their expertise, independence, and ability to represent the interests of all stakeholders in the halal industry.

Relevant Expertise

Committee members hold strong educational and technical backgrounds relevant to halal certification, food science, regulatory compliance, or related fields, giving them the knowledge needed to properly evaluate the information presented to them.

Proven Experience

Each member brings a proven track record of relevant experience, ensuring the Committee can fulfil its objectives with credibility and authority across the sectors DHCE certifies.

Stakeholder Representation

The Committee represents the interests of consumers, the trade and industry, and the broader DHCE client base, ensuring that all perspectives are considered when reviewing compliance and impartiality matters.

Analytical and Investigative Skills

Members possess the capacity to rigorously review and verify the information submitted to the Committee, allowing them to reach independent and well-reasoned conclusions on any matter brought before them.

An independent guardian of DHCE principles. The Impartiality Committee exists to protect the integrity of the certification process and to give every client, consumer, and stakeholder confidence that DHCE decisions are made with complete objectivity.
